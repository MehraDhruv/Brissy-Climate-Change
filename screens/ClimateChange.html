🌏 Climate Change – What You Need to Know

1. What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant and long-term alterations in the Earth’s climate patterns. While natural processes have always influenced climate, the current rate of change is largely driven by human activities.

These include the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas), deforestation, large-scale agriculture, and industrial emissions. These actions increase the concentration of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane (CH₄) in the atmosphere, which trap heat and result in global warming.

🌡️ “Think of greenhouse gases like a thermal blanket around the Earth — too much, and we start overheating.”

2. Why Does It Matter for Brisbane?

Brisbane is not immune to the effects of a changing climate. In fact, our city is already experiencing direct consequences that affect health, infrastructure, economy, and biodiversity.

  • Increased frequency of extreme heat events, which can lead to health emergencies, especially for vulnerable populations.
  • More intense and unpredictable storms and rainfall, resulting in flooding and damage to homes and public services.
  • Bushfire seasons are longer and more severe, threatening native bushland and wildlife.
  • Rising sea levels, placing pressure on coastal communities and ecosystems.
  • Strain on local water supplies due to shifting rainfall patterns.
🛑 Without action, Brisbane’s way of life — our parks, our rivers, our weather — could be permanently altered.

3. What Are the Causes of Climate Change?

The main drivers of climate change are tied to human progress, but their unchecked growth has led to climate instability. Key contributors include:

  • Fossil Fuel Combustion: Powering cars, factories, and homes with coal, oil, and gas.
  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests reduces the planet’s ability to absorb CO₂.
  • Industrial Agriculture: Large-scale livestock farming releases methane and nitrous oxide.
  • Overconsumption: Excessive energy use, waste generation, and fast fashion.
💭 “Every product we use and every kilometer we travel has a carbon footprint.”

4. What Are the Global and Local Impacts?

Globally:

  • Melting of polar ice and glaciers
  • Sea level rise, threatening island nations
  • Ocean acidification, impacting marine life
  • Global food insecurity from crop failures

Locally in Brisbane:

  • Hotter summers, putting pressure on health services
  • More frequent flash flooding
  • Threats to native species like koalas and birds
  • Economic losses from extreme weather damage
📊 Scientists warn that if global temperatures rise more than 1.5°C, these impacts will intensify significantly.

5. Brisbane’s Role in Fighting Climate Change

The Brisbane City Council and many community groups are already taking action to reduce our carbon footprint and build resilience:

  • Solar energy programs on rooftops across the city
  • Greener public transport initiatives (electric buses, more bike lanes)
  • Tree planting programs in parks and urban spaces
  • Climate-smart waste management including recycling and composting
  • Community workshops and education sessions to raise awareness
✅ Brisbane aims to become a carbon-neutral city in the near future — but it can only happen with your support.

6. What Can You Do? – Take Climate Action Now

Even small, everyday choices can add up to a big difference. Here’s how you can be a part of the solution:

  • 🌞 Switch to renewable energy providers for your home.
  • 🚴 Choose sustainable transport: bike, walk, or use public transport more often.
  • 🌿 Reduce meat and dairy consumption, which lowers methane emissions.
  • ♻️ Recycle and reuse materials instead of sending them to landfill.
  • 🛒 Support eco-conscious brands and local farmers.
  • 💬 Raise awareness by talking to friends, family, or on social media.
  • 💪 Volunteer or donate to local climate organizations (link to Support page).
  • 📢 Engage with council policies and vote for leaders who prioritize the environment.
🧠 “You don’t have to do everything — just start somewhere.”
